Everything I Made In Italy

Last fall, I studied abroad in Italy for four months. These 4 months helped me develop my culinary skills like no other. I got the chance to learn so much and experience the world of culinary and groceries in Italy and other countries. I was blessed to have lived right next to the San Ambrogio market with access to local fresh meats, cheeses, and produce. It was my dream and gave me so much space to cook and try things I never had in the States. I will never have access to these amazing ingredients again unless I move, so I actually may start crying. Cheers to pushing myself, learning, and experimenting.

Where to Eat in Florence, Italy

Florence, Italy, is home to a diversity of culinary pleasures ranging from high-end restaurants to tiny street-side bars perfect for smoking a Vogue alongside a morning cappuccino. Many tourists come to Italy hoping for the best pizza and pasta, but the issue is that they don’t know where to look. More importantly, they don’t know HOW to look. I wouldn’t be caught dead eating a carbonara next to the Duomo, or shopping at an “artisan truffle store” right across from the Uffizi Gallery. After living there, I managed to figure out the tips and tricks of where to eat, and compiled a list of my favorite places for every vibe.

The Original Ragu Bolognese

So… a couple years ago I worked with my Italian friend to translate the original ragu bolognese recipe from the Bologna Chamber of Commerce’s website…and dare I say it was fucking buss. This ragu will make you think differently about every other bolognese you have ever had in your life. Simmering for 30 minutes? Never heard of it. Meat melting in your mouth? Legendary. Fresh egg tagliatelle, I think you get the point. Get ready to go into the deepest food coma in your life, after devoting 4 hours to this recipe, of course.

Salmon Burgers

Sometimes you don't want to eat a big slab of meat. And that’s ok. Honestly, I don’t know why it's become antagonized to not want to eat meat for every meal. With these salmon burgers, you’re able to get your fish on. A soft salmon patty with a toasted brioche bun, crunchy fennel, arugula, and a spicy Thai basil aioli. Like holy buss. It’s time to get a little creative with it. After all, there’s really no wrong answers when it comes to dressing your burger. Unless you put American cheese on a salmon burger. That’s foul.

Recipe Ideas For Your Next Sunday Brunch

I love brunch. I think brunch is very underrated. People need to be more enthusiastic about brunch. But please don’t bullshit your brunch. Some eggs, bagels, and pancakes? Boring. Brunch needs creativity. Brunch is not just breakfast, it’s a party. It’s time to bring your controversial gluten-free blueberry protein muffins. It’s not the time to bring boxed pastries straight from Cal Fresh. Brunch is where the non-breakfast eaters get to experience something they never realized was real. Like my eggs Benedict, and sweet potato, chorizo, and rainbow chard hash.
